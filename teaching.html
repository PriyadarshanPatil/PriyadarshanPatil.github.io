General

My teaching philosophy can be found below. I have been a Graduate Teaching Assistant for two Civil Engineering courses over three semesters. The details can be found below here.

The University of Texas at Austin offers a Graduate certification for Engineering Education, providing graduate students an opportunity to improve their teaching skills by learning better teaching pedagogy, innovative methods, theoretical background about teaching principles such as argumentation, and most importantly, practical experience. I have completed the certification requirements (Fall '20). You may find information about the courses completed on the coursework page. Additionally, as a part of the Argumentation course, we (Madison Andrews, Priyadarshan Patil, Victor Sampson) have produced a research manuscipt, the abstract of which has been accepted at the ASEE Annual meeting 2021 for full manuscript consideration.

In conjunction with the Women in Engineering Program at UT Austin, I have been a graduate mentor for two sophomore students interested in obtaining research experience. I have supervised Ms. Katherine Ross (Fall '18) and Ms. Bradley Gladdens (Spring '19) on two separate research projects. My responsibilities involved introducing them to my research, providing them background reading and teaching them the basics, allowing them to conduct specific tasks/experiments/simulations and assist in designing their end-of-semester research poster. Ms. Ross and I have two presentations accepted in refereed conferences and one accepted journal publication.

CE 311: Probability and Statistics classroom teaching

I was the Teaching Assistant for CE 311 in Spring 2019. I supervised two sections with 18 and 21 students respectively. My primary resposibilities included designing a course module on introducing R and statistical analysis using R, conducting lab sessions, exam design and grading, exam review sessions and lastly, conducting office hours. You may find the official end-of-semester student feedback for the two sections here: Section 1, Section 2. My overall TA rating is 4.2 and 4.4 for the two sections.
